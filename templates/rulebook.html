RULE BOOK

Official rules and guidelines for all TechSpark events

Common Rules

📋 Applicable to ALL Events

General Rules & Regulations

  1. 1.All participants must carry their valid college ID cards at all times during the event.
  2. 2.Participants must report 30 minutes before their respective event start time.
  3. 3.Mobile phones must be kept on silent mode during all events.
  4. 4.Ragging, misbehaviour, or misconduct of any kind will lead to immediate disqualification.
  5. 5.No refunds will be given after registration under any circumstances.
  6. 6.Participants must follow all instructions given by the coordinators and volunteers.
  7. 7.Any form of cheating or malpractice will lead to disqualification of the entire team.
  8. 8.Decision of the organizing committee and judges is final and binding.
  9. 9.Participants must not damage any college property or event equipment.
  10. 10.Outsiders or non-registered individuals are not allowed inside the event area.
  11. 11.All participants must maintain discipline and decorum throughout the event.
  12. 12.Late entry will not be permitted after the event has started.
  13. 13.A participation certificate will be awarded to every registered participant.
  14. 14.TechSpark Club reserves the right to change or update rules without prior notice.

🚪 Escape Room

📅 April 21, 2026

Rules & Regulations

  1. 1.This is an intercollege event. Students from any college are welcome to participate.
  2. 2.All participants must carry their college ID cards at the time of reporting. Entry will be denied without a valid college ID.
  3. 3.Registration deadline is April 21, 2026 by 7:30 AM sharp. No registrations will be accepted after this time.
  4. 4.Participants must report by 9:30 AM on April 21, 2026.
  5. 5.The event consists of 3 rounds. Teams must complete all rounds to be eligible for prizes.
  6. 6.No mobile phones allowed inside the escape room during any round.
  7. 7.Physical damage to props or equipment will lead to immediate disqualification.
  8. 8.Teams must wait outside until called by the coordinators.
  9. 9.This is a non-technical fun activity event. No prior technical knowledge is required.
  10. 10.Decision of the judges is final and binding. No disputes will be entertained.

🐛 Bug Bounty Hunt

📅 April 22, 2026

Rules & Regulations

  1. 1.This is an inter-college event. Students from any college are welcome to participate.
  2. 2.All participants must carry their valid college ID cards at the time of reporting. Entry will be denied without ID.
  3. 3.Registration deadline is April 22, 2026 by 7:30 AM sharp. No registrations will be accepted after this time.
  4. 4.Participants must report by 9:30 AM on April 22, 2026.
  5. 5.Buggy code will be provided in C and Python languages. Participants may choose to work in either language.
  6. 6.Small intentional errors are embedded in the code. Participants must identify, fix the errors, and submit the corrected code to earn points.
  7. 7.Points are awarded for each valid bug fix. The team with the highest total points at the end wins.
  8. 8.A fun activity round will be conducted in the middle of the event.
  9. 9.Sharing findings or solutions with other teams leads to immediate disqualification.
  10. 10.Participants must identify and report bugs ethically. No destructive or malicious actions are allowed.
  11. 11.Decision of judges is final and binding. No disputes will be entertained.

⚡ Sparkathon

📅 April 23–24, 2026

Rules & Regulations

  1. 1.This is an inter-college event. Students from any college are welcome to participate.
  2. 2.All team members must be enrolled students and carry their college ID cards and laptops.
  3. 3.Teams must register before April 23, 2026 by 6:00 AM sharp. No registrations will be accepted after this deadline.
  4. 4.Participants must report by 9:30 AM on April 23, 2026.
  5. 5.This is a 24-hour hackathon. Participants are expected to stay for the full duration.
  6. 6.All code must be written during the hackathon window. Pre-built projects are not allowed.
  7. 7.Use of pre-built UI libraries is allowed; copy-pasted projects are not.
  8. 8.4 problem statements will be provided across 4 domains. Each team must select and work on exactly 1 problem statement.
  9. 9.Teams must present a working demo at the end.
  10. 10.Food will be provided: Lunch on April 23, Dinner on April 23, Tiffin on April 24, and 2 refreshment breaks.
  11. 11.Decision of judges is final and binding. No disputes will be entertained.
