Common Rule Book

Rules applicable to all TechSpark events

1. General Conduct

  1. All participants must carry a valid college ID card at all times.
  2. Participants must respect volunteers, coordinators, and fellow contestants.
  3. Any form of misconduct, cheating, or harassment will lead to immediate disqualification.
  4. Decision of the organizing committee and judges is final and binding.

2. Registration

  1. All team members must register via the official Google Form for their respective event.
  2. Registration is considered confirmed only after successful payment (where applicable).
  3. No changes to team composition are allowed after the registration deadline.

3. Punctuality

  1. Teams must report at the venue 15 minutes before the scheduled start time.
  2. Late arrivals may be denied entry without a refund.

4. Prizes & Certificates

  1. Prize money will be awarded to the top-placing teams as per each event's guidelines.
  2. Participation certificates will be issued to all registered participants who attend.
  3. Prize distribution will happen on the final day of Blackout Horizon 2.0.

5. Code of Ethics

  1. Plagiarism of any kind is strictly prohibited.
  2. Participants must not tamper with equipment, props, or event materials.
  3. Any technical issues must be reported immediately to the nearest coordinator.
