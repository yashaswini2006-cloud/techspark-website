Official rules and guidelines for all TechSpark events

Escape Room

📅 April 21, 2026

Rules & Regulations

  1. This is an intercollege event. Students from any college are welcome to participate.
  2. All participants must carry their college ID cards at the time of reporting.
  3. Registration deadline is April 21, 2026 by 7:30 AM sharp.
  4. Participants must report 30 minutes before the event start time (by 9:30 AM).
  5. The event consists of 3 rounds. Teams must complete all rounds to be eligible for prizes.
  6. No mobile phones allowed inside the escape room during any round.
  7. A participation certificate will be awarded to every participant.
  8. Physical damage to props or equipment will lead to immediate disqualification.
  9. Teams must wait outside until called by the coordinators.
  10. Decision of the judges is final and binding. No disputes will be entertained.

Bug Bounty Hunt

📅 April 22, 2026

Rules & Regulations

  1. This is an inter-college event. Students from any college are welcome to participate.
  2. All participants must carry their valid college ID cards at the time of reporting.
  3. Registration deadline is April 22, 2026 by 7:30 AM sharp.
  4. Participants must report 30 minutes before the event start time (by 9:30 AM).
  5. Buggy code will be provided in C and Python languages.
  6. Participants must identify, fix the errors, and submit the corrected code to earn points.
  7. A fun activity round will be conducted in the middle of the event.
  8. A participation certificate will be awarded to every participant.
  9. Sharing findings or solutions with other teams leads to immediate disqualification.
  10. Decision of judges is final and binding. No disputes will be entertained.

Sparkathon

📅 April 23–24, 2026

Rules & Regulations

  1. This is an inter-college event. Students from any college are welcome to participate.
  2. Teams must register before April 23, 2026 by 6:00 AM sharp.
  3. All participants must carry their college ID cards and laptops at the time of reporting.
  4. Participants must report 30 minutes before the event start time (by 9:30 AM).
  5. This is a 24-hour hackathon. Participants are expected to stay for the full duration.
  6. All code must be written during the hackathon window.
  7. Use of pre-built UI libraries is allowed; copy-pasted projects are not.
  8. Teams must present a working demo at the end.
  9. A participation certificate will be awarded to every participant.
  10. Decision of judges is final and binding. No disputes will be entertained.
